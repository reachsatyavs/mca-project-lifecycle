1. Functional Requirements

What the system must do — observable features and behaviours

A functional requirement describes a specific behaviour or function the system must provide. They are written from the perspective of what the system does, not how it does it. Each requirement is given a unique ID so it can be traced through design, coding, and testing.

FR-1: User Management

FR-101
User Registration The system shall allow new users to register using their name, email address, phone number, and password. A verification email shall be sent upon registration.
FR-102
User Login / Logout Registered users shall be able to log in using email + password. Users shall be able to log out from any page. Invalid credentials shall display an error message.
FR-103
Password Reset The system shall allow users to reset forgotten passwords via a link sent to their registered email address. The link shall expire after 30 minutes.
FR-104
Profile Management Logged-in users shall be able to update their name, phone number, and delivery addresses. Users can save multiple delivery addresses.

FR-2: Restaurant Management

FR-201
Restaurant Registration Restaurant owners shall be able to register their restaurant by providing name, address, cuisine type, opening hours, and a FSSAI licence number. Registration requires admin approval.
FR-202
Menu Management Restaurant owners shall be able to add, edit, and delete menu items. Each item shall have a name, description, price, category (e.g., Starter, Main, Dessert), photo, and veg/non-veg flag.
FR-203
Order Management (Restaurant Side) Restaurant owners shall receive a notification for each new order. They shall be able to accept or reject an order within 5 minutes. Accepted orders display preparation time.

FR-3: Ordering

FR-301
Restaurant Discovery Customers shall be able to search for restaurants by name or filter by cuisine type, rating, or price range. Results shall show restaurant name, rating, distance, and delivery time estimate.
FR-302
Cart Management Customers shall be able to add items to a cart, update quantities, and remove items. The cart shall persist across page refreshes. Items from only one restaurant may be in the cart at a time.
FR-303
Order Placement Customers shall be able to place an order by selecting a delivery address, choosing a payment method, and confirming the order. An order confirmation page and email shall be generated.
FR-304
Order Status Tracking Customers shall be able to view real-time order status transitions: Order Placed → Restaurant Accepted → Preparing → Picked Up → Out for Delivery → Delivered. Status shall auto-refresh every 15 seconds.
FR-305
Order History Customers shall be able to view a list of all past orders with date, items, total, and status. Customers shall be able to reorder any past order in one click.

FR-4: Payment

FR-401
Online Payment The system shall integrate with a sandbox payment gateway (Razorpay / Stripe test mode) to process credit card, debit card, and UPI payments. Payment failure shall allow retry.
FR-402
Cash on Delivery Customers shall have the option to pay cash upon delivery. COD orders shall display a "Collect ₹XXX" instruction to the delivery partner.
FR-403
Invoice Generation After a successful order, the system shall generate an invoice showing itemised charges, delivery fee, applicable taxes, and order total. The invoice shall be downloadable as PDF.

FR-5: Delivery Partner

FR-501
Partner Registration & Profile Delivery partners shall be able to register with name, phone, vehicle type, and licence number. Partners shall go online/offline to indicate availability.
FR-502
Order Assignment The system shall automatically assign accepted orders to the nearest available delivery partner. If no partner accepts within 3 minutes, the order is re-queued.
FR-503
Status Updates by Partner Delivery partners shall be able to mark orders as "Picked Up" and "Delivered". Each status change triggers a notification to the customer.

FR-6: Reviews & Admin

FR-601
Ratings & Reviews After delivery, customers shall be able to rate the restaurant (1–5 stars) and add a text review. Restaurant average rating is recalculated after each review.
FR-602
Admin Dashboard & Restaurant Approval The admin shall view platform-wide statistics: total orders today, revenue, active restaurants, active users. Admin shall approve or reject restaurant registrations, with an optional rejection reason sent to the owner.
FR-603
User Suspension & Ban The admin shall be able to search for any user by name or email and apply a temporary suspension or permanent ban with a reason. Suspended/banned users shall see a clear message on login. Suspensions shall be reversible by the admin.
Traceability

Every FR gets a unique ID (e.g., FR-303). When you write test cases in Semester 2, each test case links back to an FR. This is called requirements traceability — it ensures nothing slips through the cracks. Build a Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM).

2. Non-Functional Requirements

How well the system performs — quality attributes and constraints

Non-functional requirements (NFRs) define the quality of the system — not what it does but how it behaves. They are often called quality attributes or the "-ilities" (reliability, scalability, security, usability...).

Performance

NFR-1
Page Load Time Any page shall fully load within 3 seconds on a standard broadband connection (10 Mbps).
NFR-2
API Response Time All REST API responses shall return within 500 ms under normal load (up to 50 concurrent users).
NFR-3
Concurrent Users The system shall handle a minimum of 100 concurrent sessions without degradation (sufficient for a college campus scenario).

Security

NFR-4
Password Hashing User passwords shall never be stored in plain text. bcrypt (work factor ≥ 10) or Argon2 shall be used for password hashing.
NFR-5
HTTPS Enforcement All HTTP traffic shall be redirected to HTTPS. TLS 1.2 or higher shall be enforced in production.
NFR-6
Input Validation & SQL Injection Prevention All user inputs shall be validated and sanitised. Parameterised queries or ORM shall be used exclusively — no raw string-interpolated SQL.
NFR-7
Role-Based Access Control API endpoints shall enforce role-based access. A customer shall not be able to call restaurant-management APIs; a delivery partner shall not access admin endpoints.

Usability & Accessibility

NFR-8
Responsive Design The UI shall be fully functional on screens from 375 px (mobile) to 1920 px (desktop wide). No horizontal scrolling on mobile.
NFR-9
Error Messaging All form validation errors and API failures shall display human-readable, actionable messages. HTTP 500 errors shall show a friendly error page, not a raw stack trace.

Reliability & Availability

NFR-10
Uptime Target The deployed system shall maintain ≥ 99% uptime during evaluation/demo periods (measured over any 30-day window).
NFR-11
Data Backup The database shall be backed up at least once daily. Backup files shall be stored separately from the primary server.

Maintainability

NFR-12
Code Quality All source code shall follow the agreed style guide (ESLint (Airbnb config) / Prettier). Functions exceeding 50 lines shall be refactored. Code complexity (cyclomatic) shall not exceed 10 per function.
NFR-13
Logging All API requests and errors shall be logged with timestamp, user ID, endpoint, and HTTP status code. Logs shall be queryable (structured JSON format).
Tip for Students

NFRs are often ignored during development and only remembered during testing — when it's too late to fix them cheaply. Write your NFRs in Semester 1 and review them every semester. Performance NFRs directly inform your database indexing and API design decisions in Semester 2.

6. User Story vs Use Case vs Test Case

Students often mix these up — here is the clear difference

A User Story says "I want to be able to do X."  A Use Case says "Here is exactly how the system handles X, step by step."  A Test Case says "I did X — here is exactly what should happen."

One is a wish. One is a blueprint. One is a verification.

User Stories — PRD / SRS
US-01
Login to my account
As a customer, I want to log in with my email and password so that I can access my orders and saved addresses.
Acceptance criteria
  • Valid credentials grant access
  • Invalid credentials show an error
  • Session persists across page reload
US-02
Place a food order online
As a customer, I want to place a food order and pay online so that I can get food delivered without carrying cash.
Acceptance criteria
  • Cart shows item total + delivery fee
  • Payment success → order confirmed
  • Payment failure → order NOT created
US-03
Track my order in real-time
As a customer, I want to see real-time order status so that I know exactly when my food will arrive.
Acceptance criteria
  • Visual timeline with current step
  • Status auto-refreshes every 15s
  • Notification on status change
US-04
Rate and review a restaurant
As a customer, I want to rate and review a restaurant after delivery so that other users can make informed choices.
Acceptance criteria
  • Rating prompt appears after delivery
  • Only one review per order allowed
  • Avg rating updates after submission

7. Test Planning (Strategy & Scope)

What will be tested, how, and when — across all semesters

⏱ When do Test Cases actually get written and run?
Semester 1 = Test Planning (strategy, scope, template, RTM structure)
Semester 2 = Test Case Writing (formal TCs drafted from FRs and UCs)
Semester 3 = Test Case Execution (run, record PASS/FAIL, file bugs, regression)

Test planning is done in Semester 1 because it is driven by requirements — not code. You define what needs to be tested before you build it, just as you define what to build before you write code.

8. Business Rules

Policies and constraints governing system behaviour

Business rules are statements of business policy that the system must enforce. They are not functional requirements (which describe what the system does) but rather constraints on how the system should behave in specific situations. They typically come from the business domain and remain stable over time.

Order Rules

BR-01
Single Restaurant per Cart A customer's cart may contain items from only one restaurant at a time. Adding an item from a different restaurant requires clearing the existing cart.
BR-02
Minimum Order Amount The minimum cart value for a delivery order is ₹100. Orders below this amount shall not be eligible for delivery; the customer must add more items or pick up in person.
BR-03
Order Cancellation Window A customer may cancel an order only before the restaurant confirms preparation (i.e., while status is "Placed" or "Accepted"). Once status is "Preparing," cancellations are not allowed.
BR-04
Restaurant Response Timeout If a restaurant does not accept or reject an order within 5 minutes, the order is automatically cancelled and the customer is refunded.

Delivery Rules

BR-05
Free Delivery Threshold Orders with a cart value of ₹500 or above qualify for free delivery. Below ₹500, a flat delivery fee of ₹40 is charged.
BR-06
Maximum Delivery Radius Each restaurant defines its own maximum delivery radius (1–10 km). Customers whose delivery address falls outside this radius cannot order from that restaurant.
BR-07
Delivery Partner Assignment Orders are assigned to the nearest available delivery partner within 3 km of the restaurant. If no partner is available within 3 km, the search radius expands by 1 km every 2 minutes.

Payment & Refund Rules

BR-08
Refund Processing Time Refunds for cancelled online-payment orders shall be processed within 5–7 business days to the original payment instrument.
BR-09
COD Limit Cash on Delivery is available only for orders up to ₹1,000. Orders exceeding ₹1,000 must use online payment.

Review Rules

BR-10
Review Eligibility Only customers who have completed at least one order from a restaurant may leave a review. One review per order is allowed.
BR-11
Rating Calculation A restaurant's displayed rating is the arithmetic mean of all reviews. If a restaurant has fewer than 5 reviews, the rating displays as "New" instead of a star score.

Restaurant Rules

BR-12
Approval Requirement A restaurant must be approved by the admin before it appears in customer searches. Rejected restaurants may reapply after 7 days with updated information.
BR-13
Operating Hours Enforcement Customers cannot place orders at a restaurant outside its declared operating hours. The restaurant listing displays "Currently Closed" with the next opening time.
Where Business Rules Come From

Business rules are discovered through stakeholder interviews. In your project, you are both the developer and the "business owner" — so think critically: "What would Swiggy/Zomato do in this situation?" Then document it as a BR. These rules will directly drive your backend validation logic.

Deliverable for This Phase
  • SRS Document — typically 20–40 pages covering all FRs, NFRs, Use Cases, and Business Rules for your project.
  • Use IEEE 830 SRS template as a guide (Introduction, Overall Description, Specific Requirements).
  • Get SRS reviewed and signed off by faculty guide before Semester 2 design begins.
